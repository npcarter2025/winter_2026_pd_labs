================================================================================
KEY OBSERVATIONS
================================================================================

1. LINEAR RELATIONSHIP:
   - Both delays increase linearly with input slew
   - Slope ≈ 2.0 ps delay per 10 ps of input slew
   - This linear behavior is captured in Liberty timing models

2. RISE vs FALL ASYMMETRY:
   - Rise delay consistently HIGHER than fall delay
   - Difference: ~5-13 ps across all slew values
   - Indicates PMOS (pull-up) is weaker than NMOS (pull-down)

3. TRANSITION TIME BEHAVIOR:
   - Rise transitions slower than fall transitions
   - Both increase with input slew
   - Output transition times are influenced by input slew rate

4. PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION:
   - Slower input → longer time for transistors to fully turn on/off
   - Input capacitance charging time dominates at slow slews
   - PMOS has higher resistance or larger parasitic capacitance


================================================================================
MEASUREMENT DEFINITIONS (from SPICE deck)
================================================================================

fall_delay: Time from input RISING to 50% VDD to output FALLING to 50% VDD
rise_delay: Time from input FALLING to 50% VDD to output RISING to 50% VDD
rise_tr:    Time for output to transition from 20% to 80% VDD (rising)
fall_fr:    Time for output to transition from 80% to 20% VDD (falling)

These definitions are standard in digital timing characterization.
